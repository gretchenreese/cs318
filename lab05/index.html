Musings of a Europe Traveler

Entries


Paris - 12 April 2017

Paris is a beautiful city - true to the name, The City of Love, one is offered roses nearly everywhere you go. Best not accept them, as they'll cost you a pretty penny. The city itself was hazy and grey, very calm atop the Monte Martre area with the Sacre Coeur standing like a gaurdian above the city. The food is never second-best, and the fresh peaches from the produce markets were to die for. Versailles was a stop that is highly reccomended to anyone visiting Paris interested in history. The palace was garnished with golden ornaments, and the Hall of Mirrors was a specatular sight to behold.




Ireland - 13 April 2017

Ireland truly is the land of rolling green hills. A truly spectacular view indeed if one ventures to the northern coastline to see the Giant's Causeway or the carrick-a-rede rope bridge. I've not yet seen bluer waters or a more serene landscape. Moving down the cast, one of the most beautiful sights are The Cliffs of Moher, the cliffs where Ireland herself meets the sea. On a cloudy day, you're not guaranteed to see the cliffs, as the fog may even hide them. However, if you're there on a hazy or sunny day, they are indeed breathtaking. Once chilled to the bone after hiking the clifs, a stop at Fitzpatrick's pub is definitely in the cards.




Edinburgh - 14 April 2017

Edinburgh, a city well worth the history and the ghost tours. Being situated on a hill, Edinburgh has a castle almost smack in the middle of the city. From there, you can see the city itself, but also the coast of Ireland in the distance. The Royal Mile is a place well worth visiting, as it's got some of the best stops to eat in the city. Mimi's Bakehouse is a personal favourite. The ghost tours have to do with the city plagued by the Bubonic Plague - people were locked in the gated areas of the city (closes) and they are said to be haunted still until this very day.




Grantham - 15 April 2017

Grantham is a place very special to my heart. I have a second family here, and many memories locked away in a Manor House on the outskirts of the village countryside. The manor itself is located on roughly 250 acres of land, and is a house that has housed many in its many years. From WWI pilots to people like Gregory Gregory or Margaret Van Der Elst, this place is truly a museum that you can live in. If visiting though, the house is only open 6hrs per year to the general public, making it truly a secret nestled away in the hillside that even people living in Grantham their entire lives have never seen the inside of.
